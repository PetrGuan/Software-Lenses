# Hamming Mindset

> "Great work often begins by choosing the problem with unusually high leverage."

**Category:** Strategy | **Skill:** `/hamming`

## When to Use

- The team is busy, productive, and still not moving the product forward
- Roadmap prioritization and quarterly planning
- Technical debt review and prioritization
- Platform and infrastructure investment decisions
- Justifying or questioning large refactors

## Core Move

Distinguish:
- **Urgent** from **important**
- **Visible** from **high-leverage**
- **Tractable** from **strategically valuable**

Then ask: is current work improving the core system or decorating its edges?

## Diagnostic Questions

- Are we solving the real bottleneck or the most comfortable one?
- Which problem, if solved, would simplify many others?
- What important risk are we postponing because it's hard?
- Are we polishing local code while avoiding architectural debt?
- What would change if we spent this effort on the highest-leverage problem instead?

## Output Format

When analyzing with this mindset, produce:

```
Current Work Classification:
  [Each activity: urgent/important/both/neither, with leverage rating]

The Important Problem:
  [The highest-leverage problem not being worked on]

Why It's Being Avoided:
  [Cost, fear, ownership gap, uncertainty, or lack of visible payoff]

Bottleneck Analysis:
  [Which problem, if solved, would simplify the most other problems?]

Opportunity Cost:
  [What the team loses by not addressing the important problem]

Recommendations:
  [What to stop, start, and continue — with a concrete first step]
```

## Anti-Patterns This Catches

- Optimizing local code while ignoring structural debt
- Choosing tasks by comfort and visibility, not impact
- Deferring the hard problem indefinitely
- Busy teams that never move the needle
- "We'll get to it later" for problems that compound

## Pairs Well With

- **Einstein** — Hamming questions priorities, Einstein questions assumptions
- **Deming** — Hamming asks "is this the right problem?", Deming asks "is this the right system?"
- **Popper** — Hamming identifies what matters, Popper tests whether the solution actually works

## When Not to Over-Apply

- The task is intentionally small and tactical
- Chasing the biggest problem would stall concrete delivery entirely
- The team is already working on the most important thing
